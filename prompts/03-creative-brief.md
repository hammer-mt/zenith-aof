# Creative Brief — Mother's Day Campaign

You are a senior strategist developing the creative brief for Lumina Skin's Mother's Day campaign. Your job is to synthesize scattered inputs into a clear creative direction.

## Your Task

Cross-reference these 4 sources into ONE actionable creative brief:

1. **Competitor Intel:** `competitor_ads.md` (what GlowLab et al are doing)
2. **Brand Guidelines:** `brand_guidelines.md` (what we can/can't do)
3. **Audience Research:** `audience_research.md` (who we're talking to)
4. **Past Performance:** `past_creative_performance.md` (what worked before)

Read ALL files before writing.

## Output: Creative Brief

### Campaign Overview
- Campaign: Mother's Day 2026
- Flight: April 28 — May 11, 2026
- Budget: $180-220K (TBD)
- Objective: Drive gift purchases + secondary self-purchase

### Competitive Landscape
Based on `competitor_ads.md`:
- What competitors are saying/doing for Mother's Day
- White space opportunities (what no one is doing)
- Territory to avoid (already claimed)

### Audience Strategy
Based on `audience_research.md`, for each relevant segment:
- What Mother's Day means to them
- Gift-giver vs. self-purchase dynamics
- Messaging angle for this segment
- Media/channel preference

### Creative Territories
Propose 3 distinct creative directions, each with:
- Territory name
- Core insight/tension
- Sample headlines (3 per territory)
- Visual direction
- Why this will work (cite `past_creative_performance.md`)
- Risk/consideration

### Mandatories & Guardrails
Based on `brand_guidelines.md`:
- Must-haves (tone, visual, claims)
- Must-avoids (competitor refs, discount language, etc.)
- Compliance notes (FDA, disclosures)

### Format & Deliverables
Recommended ad formats based on past performance:
- Primary formats (with rationale)
- Secondary formats (for testing)
- Creator/UGC needs (if any)

### Success Metrics
Based on historical performance:
- Target ROAS
- Target CPA by segment
- Engagement benchmarks
- Comparison to Mother's Day 2025

## Guidelines
- Be specific — cite the source documents
- Recommend boldly, but note risks
- Connect insights across documents
- Keep under 1000 words

## Begin
Read all files in this directory, then produce the brief.
