# Lumina Skin — Mother's Day Campaign Concepts
## Creative Concepts for Testing (due: March 25, 2026)

### Campaign Objective
Drive gift purchases for Mother's Day (May 11, 2026). Secondary objective: self-purchase ("treat yourself" angle).

### Target Segments
- Primary: Gift-givers (daughters, partners) for Skincare Sophisticate moms
- Secondary: Self-purchasers ("I deserve this") from all segments

### Budget
$180K-$220K total media spend (pending Sarah's approval on high option)

---

## Concept A: "She Taught You Everything — Except How to Thank Her"

**Format:** UGC-style video (30s) + static carousel

**Visual Approach:**
- Opens on childhood photos morphing into present-day mom/daughter
- Mom's skincare routine intercut with daughter's memories
- Product set as hero at the end

**Headlines:**
- "The woman who gave you everything deserves a routine that gives back."
- "She taught you sunscreen. Now teach her Lumina."
- "Mom didn't ask for much. Give her more."

**CTA:** Shop Mother's Day Sets

**Hook (first 3s):** Childhood photo zoom with text "She never asked for anything..."

**Tone:** Emotional, nostalgic, heartfelt

---

## Concept B: "This Year, Skip the Flowers"

**Format:** Video (15s) + static

**Visual Approach:**
- Split screen: wilted flowers vs. glowing skin
- Bold text overlays
- Product set with premium unboxing moment

**Headlines:**
- "Flowers die. Glowing skin doesn't."
- "This year, skip the flowers. Give her something that lasts."
- "The gift she'll actually use."

**CTA:** Shop the Gift Set

**Hook (first 3s):** Wilting flower time-lapse with text "Flowers last a week..."

**Tone:** Bold, direct, slightly cheeky

---

## Concept C: "The Mom Who Has Everything (Except Time for Herself)"

**Format:** Carousel (5 cards) + video (30s)

**Visual Approach:**
- Day-in-the-life of busy mom
- Stolen moments of self-care
- Routine as "me time"

**Headlines:**
- "For the mom who puts everyone else first."
- "Give her 10 minutes of peace (and really good skincare)."
- "She schedules everyone. Help her schedule herself."

**CTA:** Give Her a Routine

**Hook (first 3s):** Calendar chaos visual with text "Her schedule is full. Her skincare cabinet shouldn't be empty."

**Tone:** Empathetic, understanding, permission-giving

---

## Concept D: "From One Generation to the Next"

**Format:** Video (45s)

**Visual Approach:**
- Three generations: grandmother, mother, daughter
- Each showing their skincare moment
- Legacy/tradition angle

**Headlines:**
- "Good skincare runs in the family."
- "She taught you more than she knows."
- "Three generations. One routine."

**CTA:** Start the Tradition

**Hook (first 3s):** Grandmother's hands opening a jar, zoom to reveal same jar 3 generations later

**Tone:** Warm, legacy-focused, generational

---

### Questions for Simulation

1. Which concept will resonate most with gift-givers?
2. Which has the highest self-purchase potential?
3. Are any concepts at risk of alienating segments?
4. Which hook will drive highest video completion?
5. Any concerns about concept execution or messaging?

---

### Creative Team Notes

- Concept A needs strong casting to land emotionally — risk of cheesy
- Concept B is bold but might offend the "flowers are lovely" crowd
- Concept C plays it safe — might not break through
- Concept D requires production complexity (3 generations) — budget implications
