# Lumina Skin — Audience Research Summary
## Q4 2025 Study + Ongoing Customer Insights

### Core Audience Segments

---

## Segment 1: "Skincare Sophisticate" (45% of customers)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 32-48
- Income: $100K-180K household
- Location: Urban/suburban, coasts over-indexed
- Education: Bachelor's degree+

**Psychographics:**
- Has tried many brands, knows what she wants
- Reads ingredient labels, does research
- Values quality over price (but not unlimited budget)
- Skeptical of marketing claims, wants proof
- Busy professional or parent, needs routine that works

**Media Behavior:**
- Instagram primary, increasingly TikTok
- Podcast listener (wellness, business, culture)
- Avoids Facebook for personal use
- Responds to long-form content (blog posts, YouTube reviews)

**Purchase Drivers:**
1. Clinical evidence / dermatologist endorsement
2. Premium ingredients at fair price (vs. "luxury markup")
3. Sustainable/ethical brand practices
4. Peer recommendations (especially from trusted creators)

**Barriers:**
- "I've tried everything, will this really be different?"
- "How do I know the clinical studies are legit?"
- "Subscription fatigue — don't want another auto-ship"

**Messaging That Resonates:**
- "Finally, skincare that's as smart as you are."
- Specific results with timeframes
- Transparency about what's in it and why
- Permission to invest in herself

---

## Segment 2: "Routine Seeker" (30% of customers)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 26-35
- Income: $60K-100K household
- Location: Diverse, slight urban skew
- Education: Mixed

**Psychographics:**
- Overwhelmed by options, wants to be told what to do
- Knows skincare matters, doesn't know where to start
- Influenced by social proof (reviews, "everyone's using this")
- Value-conscious but will pay for perceived quality
- Looking for a "system" that simplifies decisions

**Media Behavior:**
- TikTok and Instagram equally
- Heavy YouTube watcher (tutorials, routines, "get ready with me")
- Responsive to influencer recommendations
- Engages with educational content

**Purchase Drivers:**
1. Clear routine/system (not à la carte products)
2. Visible before/after results from real people
3. Creator/influencer endorsement from someone she trusts
4. Manageable price point for full routine

**Barriers:**
- "I don't understand all these ingredients"
- "Is this going to be complicated?"
- "What if it doesn't work for my skin type?"

**Messaging That Resonates:**
- "Your complete routine, simplified."
- Step-by-step guidance
- "Here's exactly what to do and when"
- Skin type quiz / personalization

---

## Segment 3: "Results-Driven" (25% of customers)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 42-58
- Income: $120K+ household
- Location: Suburban, national spread
- Education: Bachelor's+

**Psychographics:**
- Focused on anti-aging specifically
- Willing to pay for results, price less sensitive
- Has probably tried clinical treatments (Botox, peels)
- Wants products that complement professional treatments
- Less trend-driven, more loyalty once committed

**Media Behavior:**
- Facebook still relevant
- Instagram (less TikTok)
- Reads articles and reviews (Allure, Byrdie)
- Email responsive (higher open rates than younger segments)

**Purchase Drivers:**
1. Visible anti-aging results
2. Dermatologist recommendation
3. Clinical study evidence
4. Premium packaging and experience

**Barriers:**
- "I've already tried the expensive stuff"
- "Why not just get Botox instead?"
- "How is this different from my La Mer?"

**Messaging That Resonates:**
- "Clinical-grade results at home"
- "What your dermatologist would recommend"
- "The investment that's worth it"
- Specific anti-aging claims (wrinkles, firmness, brightness)

---

### Cross-Segment Insights

**Universal Truths:**
- Authenticity beats polish (real results > stock photos)
- Ingredients matter more than ever (thank you, The Ordinary)
- Sustainability is expected, not differentiating
- Subscription skepticism is real — offer flexibility

**Generational Differences:**
| Factor | Gen Z/Young Millennial | Older Millennial/Gen X |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Primary channel | TikTok | Instagram/Facebook |
| Content preference | Short, authentic, UGC | Polished + educational |
| Proof point | Viral moment, creator endorsement | Clinical study, derm rec |
| Price sensitivity | Higher | Lower |
| Decision timeline | Fast (impulse) | Slow (research) |

**Seasonality:**
- Spring: "Refresh, renew, post-winter damage repair"
- Summer: "Protect, hydrate, minimal routine"
- Fall: "Serious skincare season begins"
- Winter: "Repair, intensive treatments, gift sets"

---
*Research source: Quant survey (n=1,200) + qual interviews (n=24)*
*Study conducted: October-November 2025*
