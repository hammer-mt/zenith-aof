# WBS: VitalFit Supplements — Spring Launch

**Client:** VitalFit (sports nutrition, DTC brand, first time working with this agency)
**Budget:** $400K | **Duration:** 10 weeks (plan for 11-12 actual) | **Channels:** Meta, TikTok, Google Search, Connected TV

---

## Summary

This is a 4-channel spring launch campaign for a new DTC client, most closely matching the GlowUp Holiday Campaign in scope (4 channels, new client, ~$400K budget, 10-week target). The project follows the standard 4-phase structure (Setup, Launch, Optimize, Wrap) with a 3-week Setup driven by channel count and new-client onboarding — matching GlowUp's pattern exactly.

Three elements make this project riskier than either past reference. First, Connected TV is a channel the agency has never run for a DTC client, which means no internal playbook for setup, measurement, or vendor reconciliation. Second, creative is being produced by an external agency the team has not worked with before, introducing coordination risk on asset delivery and revision cycles — FreshBrew's 3-day brand asset delay and 2-day YouTube creative delay both originated from client-side handoff friction, and an unknown external partner amplifies that risk. Third, supplement advertising requires a legal/compliance review for health claims, which is a step that appears in neither past project. This is the single biggest scheduling wildcard because legal review timelines are unpredictable and sit on the critical path before any ad can go live.

The critical path runs: Kickoff -> Audience Research -> Media Plan -> Legal Review of Ad Copy -> Trafficking -> Phased Launch. The legal review step is the most likely bottleneck because it has no historical precedent and blocks every channel's go-live. The secondary risk is CTV setup and measurement: Marcus will be building the CTV playbook from scratch, and the client is already nervous about measurement, so expect extra cycles on reporting frameworks. Budget concentration (50% on TikTok) means mid-flight rebalancing has less room to maneuver if TikTok underperforms against the aggressive $22 CAC target.

## Recommendations

1. **Run legal review concurrently with media planning, not sequentially.** The legal/compliance step is brand new to the agency's workflow and sits on the critical path. If Sarah can get draft ad copy to legal by early Week 2, the review can run in parallel with Jordan's media plan build rather than blocking trafficking in Week 3. Every day saved on legal review is a day of buffer for launch.

2. **Treat CTV as a bounded experiment, not a full channel launch.** The client is already nervous about CTV measurement, and the agency has no DTC CTV baseline. Frame it explicitly as a test with a fixed budget cap (suggest 10-15% of total), a pre-defined measurement framework (Marcus, Week 2), and a formal go/no-go at Week 8. This protects both the client relationship and the campaign's overall performance metrics.

3. **Negotiate an SLA with the external creative agency before kickoff ends.** FreshBrew's 3-day brand asset delay and 2-day creative approval delay both came from client-side handoffs, and those were with known partners. An unknown external agency needs explicit deadlines: asset delivery by end of Week 1, first round of TikTok creator content by end of Week 2, revision turnaround of 48 hours. Put this in writing during the kickoff call.

4. **Set a TikTok-specific early warning trigger at Week 5.** With 50% of budget concentrated on a single channel and aggressive CAC targets, the standard mid-flight report in Week 6 may be too late to course-correct. Jordan should run a TikTok-only performance read at the end of Week 5. If CAC is more than 30% above target, escalate to Sarah for a client rebalancing conversation before more budget is committed.

5. **Plan internally for 11 weeks and hold Week 11 as contingency.** Both FreshBrew (planned 8, actual 9) and GlowUp (planned 10, actual 11) overran by one week, and those projects had fewer unknowns. With a new channel, new creative partner, and a new compliance step, this project has a higher probability of slippage. Communicate 10 weeks to the client but staff and schedule for 11. If things go smoothly, the team gets a week back; if they don't, the project still lands on time.

6. **Document the CTV and legal review workflows as agency playbooks after wrap.** Neither of these processes exists in the agency's historical reference base. Whatever Marcus learns about DTC CTV setup, measurement, and vendor reconciliation — and whatever Sarah learns about supplement compliance review — should be captured as reference material for future projects. This is how GlowUp's staggered launch pattern became a reusable template, and the same should happen here.
