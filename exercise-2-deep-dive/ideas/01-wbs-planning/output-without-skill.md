## Uncertainty Flags

1. **Connected TV (CTV) is entirely new territory.** The agency has no DTC CTV playbook. Marcus will need to build the deal structure, measurement framework, and reporting methodology from scratch. There is meaningful risk of underperformance or inconclusive results. Recommend ringfencing CTV budget at no more than 10-15% until proof of concept is validated.

2. **External creative agency is an unknown partner.** Every past project used internal or known creative resources. Misaligned file specs, slow revision cycles, or incompatible formats could delay launch and refreshes. The FreshBrew project already showed a 2-day delay from creative approval issues with a *known* partner -- an unknown one amplifies this risk.

3. **Legal/compliance review for supplement claims has no precedent.** Neither past project required regulatory review of ad copy. This adds a new dependency to both the initial launch and any creative refresh. If legal review takes longer than expected, it sits on the critical path and will delay go-live.

4. **50% budget allocation to TikTok is aggressive.** Neither past project allocated more than roughly 25% to any single non-search channel. Concentrating half the budget on TikTok with a $22 CAC target leaves little room for underperformance. If TikTok does not hit targets, mid-flight rebalancing will require rapid reallocation to other channels.

5. **Creator-driven TikTok content adds delivery variability.** Creator content does not follow the same production timelines as standard brand creative. If the external agency is managing creator relationships, the team has limited visibility into delivery schedules.

6. **Aggressive KPI targets with a first-time client.** $18 CAC on Meta and $22 CAC on TikTok are specific and ambitious. Without historical campaign data for this client, these targets are based on client expectations rather than proven benchmarks. There is risk of misalignment if early results fall short.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Schedule a pre-kickoff internal planning session (Week 0).** Given the number of new variables (new client, new channel, new creative partner, new compliance step), the internal team should align on roles, escalation paths, and contingency plans before the client kickoff.

2. **Negotiate a creative SLA with the external agency before Week 1 ends.** Define turnaround times for initial delivery, revisions, and creative refreshes. Get agreement on file formats and platform specs (especially CTV and TikTok). Put it in writing.

3. **Identify legal counsel for supplement claims immediately.** Do not wait until creative is delivered. Engage legal during Week 1 so they are ready to review as soon as ad copy lands. Ask if they can provide pre-approved claim language to accelerate the process.

4. **Cap CTV spend and define a clear test-and-learn framework.** Propose to the client that CTV runs as a structured test with 10-15% of budget, a defined measurement plan, and a Week 7 go/no-go decision point. This manages the client's nervousness and protects overall campaign performance.

5. **Build a 3-day buffer into every creative-dependent milestone.** Past projects show creative delays are the norm, not the exception. With an external agency, add explicit buffer days to the timeline for launch, TikTok go-live, and the Week 7 creative refresh.

6. **Set up a shared dashboard with the client by Week 4.** First-time clients benefit from transparency. A real-time or weekly-updated dashboard showing channel-level CAC against targets will reduce ad-hoc reporting requests and build trust early.

7. **Plan the mid-flight report (Week 6) as a formal client checkpoint.** Use it to present early CTV learnings, validate TikTok CAC trajectory, and get client alignment on any budget reallocation before the creative refresh in Week 7.
