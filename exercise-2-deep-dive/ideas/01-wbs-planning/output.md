# WBS: VitalFit Supplements — Spring Launch

**Client:** VitalFit (sports nutrition, DTC brand, new client)
**Budget:** $400K | **Duration:** 10 weeks | **Channels:** Meta, TikTok, Google Search, Connected TV

---

## Summary

This is a 4-channel, 10-week campaign for a new DTC supplements client. The structure follows the agency's standard 4-phase workflow (Setup, Launch, Optimize, Wrap), but this project has several first-time elements that add risk: Connected TV (new channel for DTC at this agency), an external creative partner the agency hasn't worked with, a legal compliance step for supplement claims, and a heavy creator-driven TikTok allocation at 50% of budget.

The critical path runs through legal review and external creative delivery — both are new dependencies that don't appear in past projects and could block trafficking if not started in Week 1. CTV is being treated as a formal test-and-learn pilot with a dedicated go/no-go checkpoint at Week 6. Vendor reconciliation is budgeted at 2 weeks based on historical overruns.

Six tasks are flagged as deviations from past project patterns. The biggest scheduling risk is the legal review serializing against trafficking in Week 3.

| Flag | Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------|------------|
| **CTV is brand new for the agency (DTC context)** | No internal benchmarks for pacing, CPMs, or attribution. Marcus is capable but flying without a playbook | Start conservative, build a dedicated CTV early-read checkpoint at Week 6, and document everything for future projects |
| **External creative agency (unknown partner)** | Asset delays, format mismatches, or misaligned creative quality — FreshBrew had a 2-day delay with an internal creative partner | Establish asset specs + delivery SLA at kickoff. Request creative refresh assets 1 week before needed. Sarah to own the relationship |
| **Legal review for supplement claims** | Entirely new compliance step — could block ad copy and delay trafficking if not started early | Begin legal review in Week 1 in parallel with audience research. Don't let it become a Week 3 bottleneck |
| **TikTok at 50% of budget ($200K) — creator-driven** | Past TikTok work (GlowUp) was not creator-heavy. Creator sourcing, briefing, and content approval is a different workflow | Dedicate planning time in Weeks 2-3. Jordan should own a creator content calendar separate from the media plan |
| **Aggressive CAC targets ($18 Meta / $22 TikTok)** | No historical data from this client. DTC supplements is a competitive category | Set expectations at kickoff that Weeks 4-5 are a learning phase. Mid-flight report should include a realistic CAC trajectory, not just a snapshot |
| **CTV measurement / attribution** | Client is already nervous. If reporting is unclear, trust erodes on a channel that's eating budget | Define measurement methodology before launch (Week 2-3). Anika and Marcus co-own this. Present it to the client before go-live |

## Recommendations

1. **Start legal review immediately.** This is new and unpredictable. Run it in parallel with everything in Week 1 — don't let it serialize against trafficking.

2. **Treat CTV as a formal test-and-learn pilot.** Set a CTV-specific budget ceiling (suggest 10-15% / $40-60K), define success criteria up front, and include a Week 6 go/no-go gate. This protects the client and the agency.

3. **Get the external creative agency on a call during Week 1.** Don't wait for assets to trickle in. Align on formats, specs, timelines, and the creative refresh schedule. Past projects show creative delays are the #1 cause of slippage.

4. **Build a TikTok creator content calendar as a separate workstream.** 50% of budget on creator-driven TikTok is essentially a mini content production project inside the media campaign. It needs its own timeline and checkpoints.

5. **Plan for a 2-week vendor reconciliation.** With 4 vendors (including a new CTV partner), this will take longer than a 3-channel project. Both FreshBrew and GlowUp ran over on reconciliation — assume it here too.

6. **Use this project to build the agency's CTV-for-DTC playbook.** Document setup, measurement decisions, and outcomes. The next project with CTV will be dramatically faster if this one is well-documented.
