# Lumina Skin — Historical Performance on Similar Campaigns
## Reference for Mother's Day 2026 Planning

### Previous Mother's Day Campaign (2025)

**Budget:** $145,000
**Period:** April 28 - May 11, 2025
**ROAS:** 3.8x overall

**Concept Tested:**

| Concept | Format | Spend | ROAS | CPA | Learning |
|---------|--------|-------|------|-----|----------|
| "The Gift of Glow" | Carousel | $52,000 | 4.2x | $12.50 | Winner — gift set focus, premium unboxing |
| "Thank You, Mom" | Video (30s) | $48,000 | 3.5x | $15.20 | Emotional resonated but lower conversion |
| "She Deserves It" | Static | $28,000 | 3.2x | $16.80 | Generic, didn't break through |
| "Mom's Morning Routine" | UGC | $17,000 | 4.5x | $11.80 | Small test but highest performer |

**Key Learnings from 2025:**
1. Gift-givers respond to "curated set" messaging better than à la carte
2. Premium packaging shots drove 40% higher CTR than product-only
3. Emotional hooks work for attention but need practical CTA to convert
4. Male gift-givers engaged surprisingly well with "perfect gift" positioning
5. Self-purchase messaging ("treat yourself") underperformed vs. gift focus

### Valentine's Day 2026 (Recent)

**Budget:** $95,000
**Period:** February 1-14, 2026
**ROAS:** 2.8x (underperformed target of 3.2x)

**What Underperformed:**
- "Self-love" angle was too generic
- Competed with jewelry/flowers in feed
- Gift-giving angle to partners didn't resonate (skincare too personal)

**What Worked:**
- "Galentine's" angle (friends gifting friends) — 3.4x ROAS
- Self-purchase with "you deserve this" performed better than partner-gift

**Lesson for Mother's Day:**
- Mother as gift recipient is clearer than romantic partner
- Avoid generic "love yourself" — be specific

### Holiday 2025 (Gift Season Benchmark)

**Budget:** $320,000
**Period:** November 15 - December 24, 2025
**ROAS:** 4.5x (best of year)

**Top Performing Concepts:**

| Concept | ROAS | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| "Gift of Glow" Set | 5.2x | Premium set with holiday packaging |
| "Last Minute Luxe" (final week) | 4.8x | Urgency + guaranteed delivery |
| "The Only Gift She Wants" | 4.3x | Bold positioning, specific |
| "Stocking Stuffers" (low AOV) | 3.1x | Traffic driver but low margin |

**Key Learnings:**
- Urgency messaging works at end of window
- Premium positioning > discount positioning
- Gift sets outperform individual products 2:1
- Male gift-givers need explicit guidance ("this is what she wants")

### Gift-Giver vs. Self-Purchase Performance

| Campaign | Gift-Giver ROAS | Self-Purchase ROAS | Ratio |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Mother's Day 2025 | 4.0x | 2.8x | 1.43x |
| Valentine's 2026 | 2.5x | 3.0x | 0.83x |
| Holiday 2025 | 4.8x | 3.9x | 1.23x |

**Insight:** Gift-giver messaging outperforms on designated "gift" holidays (Mother's Day, Christmas). Self-purchase wins on "love" holidays where gift recipient is ambiguous.

### Competitive Reference: What Worked for GlowLab Mother's Day 2025

Based on ad library research:

- Heavy UGC from actual mother-daughter pairs
- Testimonial: "I got my mom into GlowLab" angle
- Quiz funnel: "Find the perfect gift for her skin type"
- Aggressive first-order discount (25% off)
- Estimated spend: $200K+

**What We Should Avoid:**
- Don't match their discount (race to bottom)
- Don't copy UGC style directly (will look derivative)

**What We Could Learn:**
- Mother-daughter relational angle resonated
- Quiz/personalization as gift-selection tool
