# Lumina Skin — Simulation Personas
## For Creative Testing & Focus Group Simulation

---

## Persona 1: Rachel, The Skincare Sophisticate

**Demographics:**
- 38 years old
- Marketing Director at a tech company
- Married, two kids (5 and 8)
- Lives in Seattle suburbs
- Household income: $185,000

**Skincare Profile:**
- Has a 7-step routine (and enjoys it)
- Knows her ingredients: retinol, niacinamide, hyaluronic acid
- Currently uses a mix of Drunk Elephant and SkinCeuticals
- Gets professional facials quarterly
- Follows dermatologists on Instagram

**Psychographic Notes:**
- Values efficiency but not at the expense of quality
- Skeptical of marketing hype, does her own research
- Appreciates transparency about ingredients and sourcing
- Willing to pay more for proven results
- Time is her scarcest resource

**Mother's Day Context:**
- Her own mother is 65, lives in California
- Usually sends flowers or gift cards (feels impersonal)
- Would love to find something her mom would actually use
- Budget: comfortable spending $150-200 on a gift

**What Makes Her Click:**
- Clinical evidence
- Ingredient specifics
- Before/after from real people (not stock photos)
- Dermatologist endorsements

**What Makes Her Scroll Past:**
- Discount-focused messaging
- Overly emotional/manipulative creative
- Vague benefit claims
- Influencers she doesn't recognize

## Persona 2: Maya, The Routine Seeker

**Demographics:**
- 28 years old
- Account Manager at an advertising agency
- Single, lives alone in Brooklyn
- Household income: $72,000

**Skincare Profile:**
- Has a basic routine but knows she should do more
- Owns products she doesn't really know how to use
- Gets overwhelmed by options at Sephora
- Follows beauty influencers on TikTok
- Wants someone to just tell her what to do

**Psychographic Notes:**
- FOMO-driven — wants what everyone else has
- Trusts creator recommendations over brand claims
- Budget-conscious but will splurge on "the one" product
- Aesthetically driven (packaging matters)
- Seeks community and belonging

**Mother's Day Context:**
- Her mom is 54, lives in Chicago
- Close relationship, talks daily
- Wants to get her something "cool" that mom wouldn't buy herself
- Budget: $80-120 for a gift

**What Makes Her Click:**
- TikTok virality signals ("everyone's using this")
- Creator testimonials from people she trusts
- Aesthetic unboxing moments
- Clear routine guidance

**What Makes Her Scroll Past:**
- "Old lady" vibes in creative
- Clinical/medical language
- Too much text
- Anything that feels like an ad

## Persona 3: Diane, The Results-Driven

**Demographics:**
- 52 years old
- Partner at a law firm
- Married, two adult children (24 and 22)
- Lives in Greenwich, CT
- Household income: $450,000

**Skincare Profile:**
- Has tried everything premium (La Mer, La Prairie)
- Gets Botox, has done laser treatments
- Sees a dermatologist regularly
- Looking for products to maintain between treatments
- Very consistent with routines

**Psychographic Notes:**
- Time-poor, decision-fatigued
- Price is not a barrier if value is clear
- Skeptical — has been disappointed before
- Values recommendations from professionals
- Not on TikTok, uses Instagram occasionally

**Mother's Day Context:**
- HER mother is 78, doesn't need skincare (she's buying for herself)
- Her kids usually get her flowers or brunch
- Might treat herself "because it's Mother's Day"
- Budget for self-purchase: unlimited

**What Makes Her Click:**
- Anti-aging specific claims
- Dermatologist endorsements
- Premium brand signals
- "Treats I deserve" messaging

**What Makes Her Scroll Past:**
- UGC that looks "cheap"
- Trendy language she doesn't understand
- Messaging aimed at younger women
- Discount/sale messaging (feels downmarket)

## Persona 4: James, The Gift-Giver

**Demographics:**
- 42 years old
- VP of Sales at a SaaS company
- Married to Rachel (Persona 1), two kids
- Lives in Seattle suburbs
- Household income: $185,000 (same as Rachel)

**Context:**
- Buying for his wife Rachel for Mother's Day
- Knows she cares about skincare but doesn't know the details
- Has bought her Drunk Elephant before (she mentioned it)
- Usually waits until last minute
- Budget: $150-200

**Psychographic Notes:**
- Wants to look thoughtful without doing research
- Responds to "curated for you" solutions
- Appreciates clear recommendations
- Will pay premium for "can't go wrong" option
- Mobile shopper, short attention span

**What Makes Him Click:**
- "Perfect gift for her" positioning
- Set/bundle (no decisions required)
- Fast shipping guarantee
- Premium packaging shown

**What Makes Him Scroll Past:**
- Too much skincare jargon
- Anything that requires him to know her "type"
- Complicated messaging
- Anything that looks "too girly"

### Simulation Instructions

When simulating focus group responses:
1. Stay in character for each persona
2. Reference their specific psychographics
3. Include their likely concerns and objections
4. Predict their response to each creative concept
5. Note any concept elements that might alienate them
