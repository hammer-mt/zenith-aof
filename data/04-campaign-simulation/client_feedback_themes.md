# Lumina Skin — Client Feedback History on Creative
## Compiled from creative reviews, meeting notes, and Slack

### Sarah Chen (CMO) — Key Themes

**Consistent Feedback:**

1. **"Don't train customers to wait for sales"**
   - First mentioned: October 2025 (post-holiday debrief)
   - Mentioned again: January 2026, February 2026
   - Context: Concerns about discount-focused creative eroding brand equity
   - Implication: Avoid leading with price in Mother's Day creative

2. **"I want to feel premium, not precious"**
   - First mentioned: Brand review January 2026
   - Context: Pushing back on overly delicate/soft creative
   - Wants: Confidence and boldness, not fragility
   - Implication: "Skip the flowers" concept might actually align well

3. **"Show me real results, not promises"**
   - Repeated feedback on testimonial creative
   - Wants: Before/after, customer proof, clinical backing
   - Avoids: Vague benefit claims
   - Implication: Any emotional hook needs practical result payoff

4. **"Don't be afraid to be specific"**
   - Feedback on Q4 campaign refresh
   - Pushback on "good skin" — wants "visibly brighter in 4 weeks"
   - Implication: Specific claims > generic benefits

**Known Sensitivities:**
- Hates when competitors are referenced even obliquely
- Sensitive to anything that could feel "anti-aging in a fear-based way"
- Dislikes overly polished influencer content (prefers authenticity)
- Wants to see diverse casting (age, ethnicity) in all creative

### Mike Rodriguez (Brand Director) — Key Themes

**Consistent Feedback:**

1. **"This doesn't feel like Lumina"**
   - Trigger: Creative that's too loud, too salesy, or too trend-chasing
   - What he means: Needs warmth, sophistication, confidence
   - Implication: Any concept needs to pass the "brand filter"

2. **"Can we make the product the hero?"**
   - Prefers: Product beauty shots, texture, unboxing
   - Avoids: People-only content where product is afterthought
   - Implication: Even emotional concepts need strong product moment

3. **"Our brand guidelines were written for Instagram"**
   - Said in March 2026 meeting (re: TikTok)
   - Acknowledgment that rules need to flex for channel
   - Implication: More latitude on TikTok, tighter on Instagram/Facebook

4. **Typography and color adherence**
   - Strict on brand colors (especially avoiding red/neon)
   - Serif headlines, sans-serif body
   - Implication: Any static creative needs brand team approval

**Known Sensitivities:**
- Protective of brand voice
- Pushes back on UGC that looks "low quality"
- Wants premium packaging featured prominently
- Dislikes comparison language (even positive)
