# Audience Research — Greenfield Bank Target Segments

## Source: Q4 2025 Customer Research Study (n=2,400) + Ongoing Analytics

---

## Segment 1: Ambitious Millennials (Primary)

### Demographics
- Age: 28-38
- Income: $65K-$150K
- Life Stage: Early career peak, may have young kids or planning to
- Location: Urban/suburban, coastal skew

### Financial Situation
- Have checking/savings but feel like they should be doing more
- Some have investment accounts, many don't know where to start
- Student loan debt is a reality but manageable
- Starting to think about buying property

### Psychographics
- Career-focused, always optimizing
- Research everything before deciding
- Distrust legacy banks but skeptical of "too good to be true" fintechs
- Value efficiency — hate wasted time

### Pain Points
- "I know I should be earning more on my savings but I haven't gotten around to switching"
- "I don't trust banks that feel like tech startups playing with my money"
- "I want to feel like I'm being smart with money, not just keeping up"

### What They Want to Hear
- Proof it works (social proof, numbers)
- That switching is easy
- That their money is actually safe
- That this is a smart choice, not a risky one

### Media Behavior
- YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn
- Podcast listeners
- Email responsive if relevant
- Ad-skeptical but will engage with value

## Segment 2: Cautious Gen Z (Secondary)

### Demographics
- Age: 22-27
- Income: $35K-$70K
- Life Stage: Early career, building foundations
- Location: Urban, rent-focused

### Financial Situation
- Just starting to build savings
- May have first "real" job
- Credit score conscious
- Living paycheck to paycheck or close to it

### Psychographics
- Financially anxious (came of age during COVID economy)
- Want to do the right thing but overwhelmed by options
- Skeptical of corporations but pragmatic
- Values-driven but practical

### Pain Points
- "I want to save but I don't have much left over"
- "Every bank seems the same — what's the catch?"
- "I don't know what I don't know about money"
- "My friends use Chime but I've heard bad things about customer service"

### What They Want to Hear
- Validation that small steps matter
- Transparency about fees (actually, not marketing-speak)
- That it's designed for people starting out
- That support is real if something goes wrong

### Media Behavior
- TikTok primary, Instagram secondary
- YouTube for learning
- Influenced by peers and micro-influencers
- Will screenshot good advice

## Segment 3: Career Changers (Tertiary)

### Demographics
- Age: 35-50
- Income: Variable ($40K-$120K)
- Life Stage: Transition — new job, divorce, relocation, second career
- Location: Mixed

### Financial Situation
- May be rebuilding after a setback
- Or may be newly flush and looking for better options
- Motivated by a specific triggering event
- Re-evaluating all financial relationships

### Psychographics
- Fresh start mentality
- Doing research like a new customer
- Less brand loyal than before
- Values experience and service over flash

### Pain Points
- "I need to get my finances in order after [life event]"
- "I don't want to think about banking — I want it to just work"
- "I've been burned before, so I need to trust who I bank with"

### What They Want to Hear
- Stability and reliability
- That transitions are normal and supported
- That real humans will help if needed
- Clear paths to common goals (emergency fund, new budget)

### Media Behavior
- Google search when motivated
- Facebook (yes, still)
- Email highly responsive
- TV/CTV receptive

---

## Cross-Segment Insights

### Universal Truths
1. **Trust is earned, not claimed.** All segments skeptical of marketing-speak.
2. **Switching friction is real.** Even dissatisfied customers don't switch without reason.
3. **Safety is table stakes.** FDIC, security features expected — absence is disqualifying.
4. **APY matters.** Rate is a tangible differentiator that cuts through noise.

### Emotional Undercurrents
- **Fear:** Making a bad financial decision
- **Guilt:** Not doing enough with their money
- **Aspiration:** Being the kind of person who has their shit together
- **Frustration:** With banks that make things complicated

### Messaging Implications
- Lead with practical value, follow with emotional payoff
- Always answer "what's the catch"
- Don't try too hard to be cool — authenticity wins
- Respect intelligence — don't oversimplify
