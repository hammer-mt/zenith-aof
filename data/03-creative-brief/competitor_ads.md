# Competitor Ad Intelligence — March 2026
## Lumina Skin Competitive Landscape

### GlowLab (Primary Competitor)

**Recent Activity:** Launched new Vitamin C serum March 18. Heavy paid push observed.

**Ad Spend Estimate (Meta):** $180-220K/month (up from ~$150K in Feb)

**Creative Themes Observed:**

1. **"Clinical Results" Angle**
   - Before/after imagery (8-week transformation)
   - Stats overlay: "94% saw visible improvement"
   - Dermatologist endorsement badge
   - CTA: "See the science"

2. **UGC Testimonial Format**
   - Real customer video reviews
   - "I was skeptical but..." opening hook
   - Unboxing + first impression + 30-day follow-up structure
   - Heavy on TikTok and Reels

3. **Comparison Ads**
   - "Unlike other serums..." positioning
   - Ingredient comparison charts
   - Price-per-ounce value messaging
   - Directly calls out "premium brands that overcharge"

**Landing Pages:**
- Using quiz funnel ("Find your perfect routine")
- Offering 20% off first order + free shipping
- Subscription discount aggressive (additional 15% off)

**Estimated CPMs:** $12-15 (lower than Lumina's — they're casting a wider net)

---

### Drunk Elephant (Aspirational Competitor)

**Positioning:** Ultra-premium, Sephora-exclusive, cult following

**Creative Themes:**
- Product texture/sensory focus (ASMR-style)
- "Smoothie" product mixing concept
- Minimal text, high production value
- Influencer-heavy but very curated

**What They Don't Do:**
- No discounting (ever)
- No before/after transformation
- No clinical/stats messaging
- No UGC that looks unpolished

---

### The Ordinary (Price Competitor)

**Positioning:** "Clinical formulations, no frills pricing"

**Creative Themes:**
- Ingredient education (what does niacinamide actually do?)
- Extreme transparency (show the formula)
- No lifestyle imagery — just product and skin
- CTA focused on "learn" not "buy"

**Pricing Pressure:**
- Their Vitamin C is $6.80 vs Lumina's $48
- Different customer, but creates reference point

---

### Observed Industry Trends (March 2026)

1. **Short-form video dominance** — 15-30 second cuts outperforming longer content
2. **Creator authenticity > production value** — especially on TikTok
3. **Ingredient transparency** — customers want to understand what they're putting on their skin
4. **Sustainability messaging** — packaging, sourcing, cruelty-free certifications
5. **Quiz funnels** — personalization as acquisition hook
6. **Subscription-first** — brands leading with recurring revenue offers

---

### Opportunities for Lumina

1. **Differentiation gap:** Nobody in the competitive set is owning "luxury + clinical." GlowLab is clinical but not premium. Drunk Elephant is premium but not clinical. There's whitespace.

2. **TikTok authenticity:** GlowLab's UGC feels forced. Lumina's creator content (@skincarebysarah) is genuinely resonating. Double down.

3. **Ingredient storytelling:** The Ordinary educated the market on ingredients. Lumina can do the same for premium formulations — why our Vitamin C is worth 7x.

4. **Anti-discount positioning:** As GlowLab races to the bottom with offers, Lumina can hold premium positioning. "Worth the investment" narrative.
